Worldwide Interoperability for SEmantics IoT


▶ Wise-IoT ski resort IoT interworking project




2 years project June16-May 18
9 EU –9 KR partners

Context and motivation

  • Emergence of many “IoT platforms” adding to vertical silos, technology islands
  • Interoperability & interworking among these technology islands required as well as tackling gaps & overlaps

Objectives

  • Global IoT standards interoperability
  • Using interworking semantics
  • Interworking between heterogeneous IoT platforms
  • Trust and security

Use Cases

  • Conquer the slope (gamification)
  • Energy Monitoring
  • Incident rollback
  • Rich parking
  • Traffic management

Consortium

  • Europe : EGM(Easy Global Market, FR), NEC(UK), TID(Telefonica, SP), CEA(Commissariat à l’énergie atomique, FR), UC(University of Cantabria, SP), UL(University of Liverpool, UK), TSP(Telecom Sud Paris, FR), Institure of applied sciences and arts (CH)
  • Korea : Sejong University (SJU), KETI, Samsung SDS, KAIST, Kyungbook National University (KNU), etc.

Description

MySkiPerformance

-       Measure ski performance for individuals and provide various statistics (incl. grade)

-       Identify starting point (use a button or a specific movement)

-       Real time GPS tracking and delivery feature (Or deliver data at the final line to save battery)

-       Provide performance grade and statistics

-       Deliver grade and statistics to smart phone or digital sinage

KidsSkiCamp

-       Provide a tracking and location service to ski camp students and teacher

-       Location service to parents and teacher

-       Tracking service to teacher with various information including speed, the number of turns

ResortCam

-       Replace legacy ski resort cam to IoT based cam

-       Provide web and mobile version interfaces

-       Consider existing systems such as streaming server and license for playing

WattageMeter

-       Measure energy usage for snow canon and provide a dashboard

-       Use wattagemeter clip from SDS + LoRa module from SKT to transfer measured data

-       Energy usage for each snow canon and total usage including various statistics

-       Consider existing system architecture and electricity design
